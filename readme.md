

Phase 1 — Core MVP

The first app should solve one problem:

Give users a realistic fitness plan and help them follow it daily.

User features

1. Authentication

*  Email, Google or phone login 
*  User profile 
*  Consent and privacy acceptance 

2. Fitness onboarding

Ask for:

*  Weight-loss or muscle-gain goal 
*  Current weight and target weight 
*  Height and age 
*  Activity level 
*  Exercise experience 
*  Workout location 
*  Equipment 
*  Preferred workout days 
*  Diet type 
*  Allergies 
*  Cuisine 
*  Budget 
*  Cooking time 
*  Medical conditions and injuries 

3. Initial plan

Generate:

*  Daily calorie range 
*  Protein target 
*  Simple nutrition guidelines 
*  Seven-day meal plan 
*  Weekly workout schedule 
*  Exercise instructions 
*  Sets, repetitions and rest time 
*  Beginner alternatives 

Use fixed calculations and expert-created templates. Use AI mainly to personalize wording and food choices.

4. Daily tracking

Allow users to log:

*  Weight 
*  Meals 
*  Water 
*  Workout completion 
*  Steps 
*  Sleep 
*  Energy 
*  Hunger 

Keep logging extremely simple. Too many required fields will kill retention.

5. Progress dashboard

Show:

*  Current weight 
*  Seven-day weight average 
*  Weight change 
*  Workouts completed 
*  Daily protein adherence 
*  Weekly consistency 
*  Goal progress 

6. Basic notifications

*  Workout reminder 
*  Meal reminder 
*  Weight check-in 
*  Weekly summary 

Admin features

Create a small admin panel for:

*  Exercise management 
*  Meal templates 
*  Food database 
*  User management 
*  Reported safety issues 
*  Plan review 
*  Notification management 

Avoid in Phase 1

Do not build:

*  Social feed 
*  Trainer marketplace 
*  Food image recognition 
*  Exercise video analysis 
*  Wearable integrations 
*  Gamification system 
*  Live coaching 
*  Automatic budget optimization for meals 

Phase 1 success metrics

Monitor:

*  Onboarding completion 
*  First plan generation 
*  First-day check-in 
*  Seven-day retention 
*  Workout completion 
*  Meal logging frequency 
*  Weekly active users 


Phase 2 — Adaptive coaching

This is where the product starts becoming different.
The app should stop giving users a static plan and begin adapting to their behaviour.

Add weekly reviews

Every seven days, collect:

*  Average weight 
*  Waist measurement 
*  Workout completion 
*  Strength improvement 
*  Hunger 
*  Energy 
*  Sleep 
*  Difficulty following meals 
*  Injuries or pain 
*  User satisfaction 

Add automatic adjustments

The system can:

*  Increase or decrease calorie targets gradually 
*  Change workout difficulty 
*  Replace difficult exercises 
*  Reduce workout duration 
*  Increase recovery 
*  Change meal quantities 
*  Suggest easier meal options 
*  Adjust plans around user schedules 

Do not change calorie targets based on one day. Use rolling averages and minimum observation periods.

Add “Plan Repair”

This should become your primary differentiating feature.
Users can select:

*  Missed workout 
*  Ate too much 
*  Travelling today 
*  No gym access 
*  Only 15 minutes available 
*  Feeling tired 
*  Restaurant meal 
*  No planned food available 

The app repairs the day instead of showing failure.
Example:

You missed the gym. Complete this 16-minute home workout instead. Your weekly workout target remains achievable.

Add AI coach chat

The AI coach can answer:

*  What should I eat now? 
*  Can I replace this meal? 
*  Why has my weight increased? 
*  Can I train while sore? 
*  What should I order at a restaurant? 
*  I missed two workouts. What should I do? 

The AI should only use approved exercise, nutrition and safety data.

Phase 2 success metrics

*  Percentage of users completing weekly reviews 
*  Number of plans adjusted 
*  Plan-repair usage 
*  Retention after a missed day 
*  Four-week user retention 
*  Percentage of users showing measurable progress 


Phase 3 — Local food and lifestyle intelligence

This phase creates a stronger competitive advantage.

Regional food database

Build structured food data for selected markets first.
Include:

*  Local dishes 
*  Serving sizes 
*  Calories 
*  Protein 
*  Carbohydrates 
*  Fat 
*  Fibre 
*  Ingredients 
*  Vegetarian status 
*  Approximate cost 
*  Cooking time 
*  Regional availability 

Start with one country or region. Do not try to support the entire world initially.

Local meal planning

Generate plans using:

*  User city 
*  Cuisine 
*  Food budget 
*  Nearby availability 
*  Religion 
*  Vegetarian preferences 
*  Cooking equipment 
*  Cooking skills 
*  Work or college meals 
*  Seasonal ingredients 

Meal replacement engine

Every meal should include:
Replace meal
Replacement options should match:

*  Similar calories 
*  Similar protein 
*  Similar cost 
*  Similar cooking time 
*  Dietary restrictions 
*  Local availability 

Grocery planning

Add:

*  Weekly grocery list 
*  Estimated cost 
*  Ingredient quantities 
*  Already-owned ingredient selection 
*  Food-waste reduction suggestions 
*  Meal-preparation instructions 

Restaurant mode

Let users search or manually enter restaurant dishes.
Provide:

*  Better choices 
*  Portion guidance 
*  Suggested modifications 
*  Estimated calorie range 
*  Confidence level 

Phase 3 success metrics

*  Meal-plan acceptance rate 
*  Meal replacement usage 
*  Grocery-list usage 
*  Reduced meal-plan abandonment 
*  Regional food-data accuracy reports 


Phase 4 — Retention and behaviour change

Fitness products fail when users lose motivation. This phase focuses on keeping them engaged without making the app childish.

Add habit intelligence

Detect patterns such as:

*  User skips Monday workouts 
*  User overeats after poor sleep 
*  User stops logging on weekends 
*  User misses protein at breakfast 
*  User abandons long workouts 
*  User performs better with home workouts 

Then provide targeted interventions.
Example:

You completed 80% of workouts under 30 minutes but only 35% of longer sessions. Next week’s workouts will be shorter.

Add adherence score

Create a score based on:

*  Workout completion 
*  Meal consistency 
*  Protein intake 
*  Sleep 
*  Weight logging 
*  Recovery 

Do not punish users for one bad day.

Add non-scale progress

Track:

*  Waist 
*  Chest 
*  Arms 
*  Hips 
*  Strength 
*  Workout capacity 
*  Progress photos 
*  Clothing fit 
*  Energy levels 
*  Resting heart rate 

Add milestones

Examples:

*  First five workouts completed 
*  First month consistent 
*  First strength increase 
*  Waist reduction 
*  Improved sleep 
*  First successful repaired day 

Coaching personalities

Allow users to choose:

*  Supportive 
*  Direct 
*  Scientific 
*  Minimal 
*  Competitive 

Only the tone changes. The underlying recommendations should remain the same.

Phase 4 success metrics

*  Eight-week retention 
*  Reduction in abandoned plans 
*  Habit-intervention engagement 
*  Number of users returning after missed weeks 
*  Paid conversion 


Phase 5 — Camera and sensor features

Build these only after the core tracking and adaptation systems work.

Food camera

Users photograph a meal.
The system returns:

*  Detected foods 
*  Estimated portions 
*  Calorie range 
*  Protein estimate 
*  Confidence level 
*  Correction controls 

Users must be able to edit the AI result.

Menu scanner

Users photograph:

*  Restaurant menu 
*  Canteen menu 
*  Buffet 
*  Grocery shelf 

The system recommends the best available options for their current target.

Progress photo analysis

Use photos to help users compare visible changes over time.
Avoid presenting precise body-fat calculations as medical-grade measurements.

Exercise form assistance

Start with a limited number of exercises:

*  Squats 
*  Push-ups 
*  Lunges 
*  Planks 
*  Shoulder press 
*  Bicep curls 

Provide:

*  Rep counting 
*  Range-of-motion feedback 
*  Tempo feedback 
*  General posture warnings 

Do not diagnose injuries.

Phase 5 success metrics

*  Camera-result correction rate 
*  Food-recognition accuracy 
*  Percentage of camera logs completed 
*  Exercise-analysis usage 
*  User-reported usefulness 


Phase 6 — Integrations and professional support

Wearables and health platforms

Integrate with:

*  Apple Health 
*  Google Health Connect 
*  Fitbit 
*  Garmin 
*  Smart scales 

Import:

*  Steps 
*  Sleep 
*  Workouts 
*  Heart rate 
*  Weight 
*  Active calories 

Treat wearable calorie estimates cautiously.

Human expert support

Offer optional access to:

*  Certified trainers 
*  Registered dietitians 
*  Physiotherapists 
*  Health coaches 

Experts should be able to:

*  Review plans 
*  Edit plans 
*  Monitor progress 
*  Message users 
*  Receive safety alerts 

B2B product

Later, provide a coach dashboard for:

*  Gyms 
*  Fitness trainers 
*  Dietitians 
*  Corporate wellness programs 

This can become a stronger revenue stream than consumer subscriptions alone.

Phase 7 — Your long-term moat

Once you have sufficient user data, build the Fitness Digital Twin.
It should learn:

*  The user’s actual maintenance calories 
*  Which meals improve adherence 
*  Which workout duration they complete 
*  Their recovery pattern 
*  Their likely dropout triggers 
*  Their response to calorie changes 
*  Their strength progression 
*  Their preferred coaching style 

The app should provide explainable adjustments:

Your average weight decreased faster than the target range for two consecutive weeks, while your energy and training performance declined. Your daily target has been increased slightly.

This intelligence—not the chatbot—can become your defensible product.
